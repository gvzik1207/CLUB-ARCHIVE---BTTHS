CLUB ARCHIVE

Club Archive is a website specifically designed to aid students who struggle with navigating clubs found in Bacolod Tay Tung High School.

It aims to provide detailed information and help students locate their desired clubs with minimal to no trouble.

WHO ARE WE?

THE DEVELOPERS

We are a team of Grade 10 programming students learning how to create a website using HTML and CSS for our IPT.

This website is dedicated to students who struggle to find information about school clubs.

SERVIAM

Serviam serves as the official student support arm of the Community Involvement and External Linkages Office (CIELO).

Album

Rules

  1. The club is open to all students from Grades 7–12 who submit a signed commitment form and attend the official orientation.
  2. Members are expected to actively take part in all club activities, including planning, implementation, and evaluation.
  3. Members must be on time and present in all required meetings, events, and volunteer duties. Absences must be communicated in advance to the officer in charge.
  4. Members must be part of the official group chat or platform to receive updates and instructions, and are expected to respond responsibly.
  5. Members may be assigned to committees or specific roles according to their strengths and are expected to contribute wholeheartedly.
  6. Serviam members must always uphold respect, integrity, and responsibility, representing the club and school with dignity in all settings.
  7. Opportunities for leadership are earned through dedication, values-based service, and formation, not popularity or personal gain.
  8. Members must embody the spirit of volunteerism, compassion, and unity, staying true to the mission of the club and the value of Great Harmony (大同).

Programs

  • Serviam Encounter
  • Pagdihon Formation Series
  • Project Yuhom
  • Serviam Work Camp
  • Serviam Quest
  • Community Immersion
  • Involvement in various Outreaches

YOUNG DESIGNERS

The Young Designers Club is a creative space for Grades 4 to 12 students who love design, innovation, and hands-on learning.

Album

Rules

  1. Members must be committed. Three consecutive absences from group meetings will be grounds for termination of membership.
  2. Members and Leads must be responsible for their individual or team projects.
  3. Members must follow all BTTHS Rules and Regulations.
  4. The Young Designers Club shall be the youth arm of the BTTHS–Design, Research, and Innovation Learning Laboratory as official participants or student leaders in its Project-Based Learning Program. Nonetheless, the Club Members shall not be responsible for other programs or activities offered by the office, such as hosting the Culminating Activities in Elementary Research and organizing Skills Building Programs for the entire school community. This iteration is necessary to avoid overlaps in the roles and responsibilities of all persons involved.

Programs

  • Design Sprints and Innovation Challenges
  • Hands-on Maker Projects
  • Passion-Based Mini-Workshops and Skill-building Activities
  • Community Design and Outreach Initiatives
  • Showcases and Exhibitions (e.g., Maker Faire, Design Week)

YES

Album
